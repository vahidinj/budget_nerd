Your data stays yours.

Effective date: February 13, 2026

This page explains how your statement data is handled when you use MyBudgetNerd. The goal is simple: give you clarity without storing your PDFs.

PDFs stay on your system

Uploaded PDFs are parsed in-memory and are not persisted by default. Results are returned to your browser for analysis.

AI refinement is opt-in

Only sanitized transaction descriptions are shared. PDFs, account numbers, and balances do not leave the server.

You control retention

Clear local cached data at any time using the Clear action in Recent. You decide what to keep.

What happens to my PDF?

  • Your PDF is uploaded over HTTPS to the backend for parsing.
  • The server parses it in-memory and returns structured transactions to your browser.
  • By default, the PDF is not stored on the server.

What data is shared for AI refinement?

  • Only sanitized transaction descriptions are sent when AI refinement is enabled.
  • Account numbers, balances, and the PDF itself are never transmitted.
  • AI refinement can be toggled off at any time.

Local storage and caching

  • We store minimal session data in your browser to improve continuity (like recent file names).
  • No raw PDFs are stored locally by the app.
  • Use Clear in Recent to remove cached data and reset the interface.

Security posture

  • Transfers are excluded from analytics to avoid double counting.
  • Uploads are secured with HTTPS in transit.
  • Open source transparency: review the codebase on GitHub.
